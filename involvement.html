Research

We are always working on a variety of projects and looking to recruit team members to the lab (undergraduates, master's and PhD students, and post-doctoral scholars; we even host stellar high school students from time to time!) Projects seeking students are marked with a note "Recruiting students" — specific current opportunities include:

  • Biophilic Interfaces
  • EcoSketch
  • Families in Cars
  • Interactive Narratives
  • Well-Building
  • Interpersonal Informatics
  • Experiences and Representations of Time
  • Games and Toys

You may particularly appreciate and thrive at Dartmouth and Thayer if you consider yourself a "builder" on multiple levels; beyond imagining and creating innovative software and hardware, we are in the midst of rapidly expanding design research within Dartmouth and the wider research community, which offers a unique opportunity to play a role in envisioning and shaping the future of human-centered engineering and interactive, societally impactful technology.

To get involved with the lab, you can fill out our recruiting form or email Liz with a description of your research experiences and interests.

Master's students

Many of our research projects are well suited to Master's students, either as independent or collaborative work. Students can also come to us with new ideas they would like to pursue as part of the lab. Either way, a more substantial commitment (e.g., in terms of hours per week, staying involved for multiple terms) is expected of Master's students.

Dartmouth Engineering offers a variety of Master's programs with both terminal and combined degrees.

  • These options include the MS, MEng, MEM, and BE/MEM as well as the MBA/MEM, MD/MS, and MD/PhD. More details and links to applications are available here.
  • Liz is core Engineering faculty for the Master of Engineering Management (MEM) program at Dartmouth. The MEM degree is designed for students who aim to learn both the engineering and business aspects of technical systems and become leaders in organizations driven by technology.
  • Masters students in Computer Science may be interested in the MS in CS with Concentration in Digital Arts.

Postdocs

The lab accepts postdoc applications on a rolling basis. Please email Liz to describe your mutual interests and your objectives & goals from a postdoctoral position.

  • In particular, please specify if you are interested in the Neukom Postdoctoral Fellowship.

Undergraduate students

We very much enjoy engaging undergraduate students in research. To express interest in getting involved with projects in our lab, please fill out our recruiting form or email Liz to describe your relevant background and the projects that appeal to you.

Our general policy is to offer credit to students who are new to the lab. We also maintain a list (embedded below) of relevant Dartmouth-sponsored research programs that could help fund involvement with the lab; let Liz know if you want to specify her as the faculty mentor on an application:

(Jan 4, 2021): Attention sophomore and junior women! The engineering department still has Luce fellowships available, which can sponsor your research with our group. The fellowship covers 3 terms of support (total stipend of $7000) and a small amount in research supplies ($2000-$3000). To apply, you'd need to be a U.S. citizens or permanent resident (required by the foundation), have declared (or intend to declare) an engineering major, and commit to 3 terms of research: 2 "on" terms (while taking classes) and 1 "off" term (full time research). If interested, please research out to Liz who can help match you with a project and assist with preparing the application materials, which include a statement of research and a recent resume.

Coursework

If you're interested in HCD/HCI classes, consider taking ENGS12: Design Thinking or COSC67: Introduction to Human-Computer Interaction as a starting point. Here's a more comprehensive list of relevant courses:

  • ENGS 12: Design Thinking
  • ENGS 13: Virtual Medicine and Cybercare
  • ENGS 15: Senior Design Challenge
  • ENGS 69/COSC 065: Smartphone Programming
  • ENGS 75: Product Design
  • ENGS 89/90: Engineering Design Methodology and Project Initiation
  • ENGM 191: Product Design and Development
  • COSC 1 Introduction to Programming and Computation
  • COSC 21 Foundations of Digital Design
  • COSC 23.01 or 63.01: Augmented and Virtual Reality Design or Development
  • COSC 25.01 and/or 02: Intro to UI/UX Design I/II
  • COSC 29.04 Impact Design
  • COSC 29.05: Digital Fabrication & Rapid Prototyping
  • COSC 63.01 Augmented and Virtual Reality Development
  • COSC 67/267: Introduction to Human Computer Interaction
  • COSC 69.11: Mobile X
  • COSC 89.20: Data Science for Health

If these topics pique your interest, Thayer offers a Human-Centered Design Minor. In the spring term, Liz teaches ENGM191, which is geared at Thayer MEM students, Innovation PhD students, Tuck MBA students, and Dartmouth medical students.
